Individuals sometimes benefit more rapidly and profoundly from group therapeutic interventions. Frequently there is an established or common goal among members to achieve some skill set or awareness regarding social identity and self-esteem. Dynamic interactions, being “present”, and offering/receiving feedback shapes awareness and social maturity.

These valuable learning experiences rarely present in “the real world”. Individuals are initially evaluated and screened to assess their need for Group Psychotherapy, and contract to meet typically at least four sessions prior to the final or “processing” meeting. Due to the economics of Group Psychotherapy, patients often attend for months.
