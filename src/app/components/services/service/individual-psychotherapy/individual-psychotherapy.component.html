Face to face individual meetings usually lasting 60 minutes continue the evaluation process to some degree, but gradually evolve to include exploring clinical insights and sharing cognitive, emotional, and behavioral skill sets. Over time the general goal is for patients to achieve and maintain a higher sense of self-awareness thus breaking free from painful emotional cycles.

Psychotherapeutic intervention strategies and styles are designed to address the unique circumstances and characteristics for each individual. No two are alike; true psychotherapy incorporates both science and art to create meaningful and emotionally profound healing.

Treatment Modalities include but are not limited to:

  • Object-Relations and Psychodynamic perspectives
  • Gestalt experiential and somatic approaches
  • Cognitive-Behavioral training techniques and anger management
  • Hypnosis, relaxation training, and visual imagery

Psychiatric medication is sometimes an important component in the treatment of certain psychological disorders. Although these are not prescribed or managed here, collaboration with the physician is provided to coordinate services.
